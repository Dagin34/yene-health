## Nourishing Yourself and Your Growing Baby

During pregnancy, maternal nutrition is one of the most critical factors influencing both the mother's health and the baby's development. Proper nutrition helps support fetal growth, prepares the body for labor, and sets the foundation for successful breastfeeding.

Here are the essential vitamins, minerals, and dietary guidelines for a healthy pregnancy.

---

### 1. Essential Prenatal Nutrients

While a balanced diet is key, certain nutrients require special attention during pregnancy:

- **Folic Acid**: Crucial in early pregnancy (first 12 weeks) to prevent neural tube defects. Aim for 400-800 mcg daily.
- **Iron**: Needed to produce hemoglobin, which carries oxygen to the baby. Iron requirements double during pregnancy. Combine iron-rich foods with Vitamin C for better absorption.
- **Calcium & Vitamin D**: Essential for the development of your baby’s bones and teeth. If intake is insufficient, your body will draw calcium from your own bones.
- **DHA (Omega-3)**: Supports fetal brain and eye development. Found in low-mercury fish, flaxseeds, and prenatal supplements.

---

### 2. Foods to Prioritize

Ensure your meals are rich in whole, nutrient-dense foods:

- **Lean Proteins**: Chicken, fish, eggs, beans, and lentils help build baby's tissues.
- **Colorful Vegetables & Fruits**: Provide essential vitamins, fiber, and antioxidants to combat pregnancy-related constipation.
- **Whole Grains**: Oats, brown rice, and whole wheat offer sustained energy and B vitamins.
- **Healthy Fats**: Avocados, olive oil, and nuts support cell membrane development.

---

### 3. Hydration Matters

Your body requires more water during pregnancy to support increased blood volume and amniotic fluid.

- Aim for **8 to 10 glasses (approx. 2.3 liters)** of water daily.
- Proper hydration reduces the risk of urinary tract infections (UTIs), headaches, swelling, and premature labor.

---

### 4. What to Limit or Avoid

- **Raw or Undercooked Meats and Seafood**: Can contain harmful bacteria like Listeria or Salmonella.
- **High-Mercury Fish**: Avoid shark, swordfish, king mackerel, and tilefish.
- **Excessive Caffeine**: Limit to under 200mg per day (about one 12-oz cup of coffee).
- **Alcohol**: There is no known safe amount of alcohol during pregnancy. Avoid completely.
